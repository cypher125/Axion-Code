## 3. Profile Overview Structure

### First 2 Lines (Visible in search results — ~250 chars)

This is the only thing clients see alongside 20 other freelancers. Be specific, use numbers, state an outcome.

**Bad:**
> "I am a passionate and dedicated professional with over 5 years of experience delivering high-quality AI solutions to clients across various industries."

**Good:**
> "I integrate AI (ChatGPT, Claude, LLMs) into your existing business workflows and software. Last quarter I delivered 11 AI integration projects with a 100% Job Success Score — automating processes that saved clients 40+ hours/week combined."

### Full Overview Template

```
[Line 1-2: What you do + specific result with numbers]

I build production-ready AI systems for businesses — not demos.

What I deliver:
• Custom AI agent development (LangChain, LangGraph, CrewAI)
• LLM API integration (OpenAI, Claude, Gemini) into your existing stack
• RAG pipelines for document Q&A and knowledge bases
• AI workflow automation with n8n, Make.com, and Zapier
• AI-powered chatbots with real business logic

Who I work with:
SaaS companies, startups, and mid-market businesses that want AI
integrated into their actual products and workflows — not just a
ChatGPT wrapper.

My process:
1. 30-min scoping call to understand your workflow
2. Written SOW with deliverables, timeline, and milestones
3. Weekly progress demos
4. Handoff with documentation and 14-day support

Tech stack: Python, TypeScript/Node.js, LangChain, OpenAI API,
Claude API, PostgreSQL, Pinecone/Weaviate, n8n, Make.com, Docker

Message me with your project details — I reply within 12 hours
with a preliminary scope and estimate.
```

**Rules:**
- Keep total overview under 2,500 characters
- Lines 1–2: What you do + a specific result (truncated preview)
- Lines 3–5: Who you work with and what you deliver
- Lines 6–8: Your process in brief
- Final line: Clear CTA with response time promise
- Never use the word "freelancer" — clients already know

## 7. Proposal Tips — Getting Hired Once Found

### When to Bid

- [ ] Job description is specific (not "I need AI")

- [ ] Your skills match at least 80% of requirements
- [ ] Budget aligns with your rate
- [ ] Fewer than 20 proposals already submitted
- [ ] Posted less than 48 hours ago

### Proposal Structure

1. **First line** — prove you read the job post (reference something specific they said)
2. **Mini-plan** — 3 bullet points of how you'd approach their project
3. **Relevant proof** — link to a similar project or result
4. **End with a question** — drives conversation, makes you memorable

### Timing

- **Apply within 2 hours** of job posting — early proposals get significantly more views and interview invites

## 9. JSS (Job Success Score) — The Number That Controls Everything

### How JSS Works

- Calculated daily using your best score across 6-, 12-, and 24-month windows
- Factors: public feedback, **private feedback** (clients rate you privately), contract end reason
- Higher-value contracts carry more weight ($5K project > $200 project)
- Long-term contracts (90+ days with payment) are automatically counted as successful

### Thresholds That Matter

| JSS | Effect |
|---|---|
| **90%+** | Top Rated eligibility, maximum search visibility |
| 80–89% | Functional, but losing placement to higher-scored competitors |
| Below 79% | Significant visibility penalty; new clients become scarce |

### How to Protect It

- **Never ghost a contract** — one abandoned contract can tank JSS for months
- If a project goes sideways, communicate and close properly
- Aim for long-term contracts when possible (auto-count as successful)
